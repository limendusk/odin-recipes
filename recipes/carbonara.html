Carbonara

Description

A pasta dish made with fatty cured pork, hard cheese, egss, salt, and black pepper. It creates a creamy sauce using the emulsion of eggs, cheese, and pasta water, without using cream.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Cured pork (typically guanciale)
  • Eggs
  • Hard cheese (typically Pecorino Romano)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Steps

  1. Render the cured pork — Cook it in a pan over medium heat until it turns golden and slightly crispy. Set aside and keep the fat in the pan.
  2. Boil the pasta — Cook salted water until just shy of al dente. Keep a cup or two of pasta water before draining.
  3. Make the egg mixture — Whisk together eggs, yolks, and grated Pecorino Romano in a bowl. Add cracked black pepper.
  4. Combine pasta and guanciale. Add the drained pasta to the pan with guanciale fat with the heat off or very low.
  5. Add the egg mixture — Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta while tossing to combine. Add pasta water a splash at a time to loosen the sauce and help it emulsify into a creamy consistency.
  6. Adjust and serve — Add pasta water as needed, and season with salt, black pepper, and extra grated cheese to taste.
