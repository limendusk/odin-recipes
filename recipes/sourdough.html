Sourdough

Description

A type of bread made through the natural fermentation of flour and water by wild yeast and lactic acid bacteria. It has a tangy flavor, crispy texture, and a crisp, artisan crust.

Ingredients

  • Bread flour
  • Water
  • Active sourdough starter
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Feed your starter — make sure it's active and bubbly before using it.
  2. Mix the dough — combine flour, water, and starter until no dry flour remains. Let it rest for 30-60 minutes.
  3. Add salt — mix in the salt and incorporate it fully.
  4. Bulk fermenatition — let the dough rise at room temperature for 4-6 hours, performing stretch-and-folds every 30 minutes for the first couple of hours.
  5. Shape — turn the dough out, pre-shape it into a round, rest for 20-30 minutes, then do a final shape.
  6. Cold proof — place the shaped dough in a floured banneton or bowl, cover, and refrigerate overnight (8-16 hours).
  7. Preheat — heat your oven and a Dutch oven inside it to a high temperature.
  8. Score and bake — emove the dough from the fridge, score the top with a razor or knife, and bake covered in the Dutch oven for about 20 minutes, then uncovered until the crust is deep golden brown.
  9. Cool — resist cutting into it for at least an hour so the crumb sets properly.
